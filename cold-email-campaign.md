## Finding Ukrainian SMB Leads

### Free Platforms and Directories
1. **YouControl (youcontrol.com.ua)** — Ukrainian business registry with company financials, owners, and contact data. Free tier gives limited searches.
2. **OpenDataBot (opendatabot.ua)** — Open data on Ukrainian companies. Search by industry code (KVED), region, revenue range.
3. **EVO.business ecosystem** — Prom.ua, Rozetka marketplace seller directories. Scrape seller pages for store names, then find emails.
4. **DOU.ua** — Ukrainian tech community. Company profiles list leadership, employee count, and often direct contacts.
5. **Clutch.co** — Filter by Ukraine, service type. Good for IT companies segment.
6. **Ukrainian Chamber of Commerce directory (ucci.org.ua)** — Regional business listings.
7. **Google Maps scraping** — Search by business type + city (e.g., "online store Kyiv"). Use tools below to extract data.

### Paid Tools for Ukrainian Market
- **Snov.io** — Founded in Ukraine, strong local database. Email finder + verification. Free tier: 50 credits/month. Paid: from $39/month.
- **Hunter.io** — Domain search to find emails from any Ukrainian company domain. Free: 25 searches/month. Paid: from $49/month.
- **Apollo.io** — Free tier gives 10,000 credits. Filter by Ukraine, company size, industry. Best free option for volume.

## Building a List of 500-1000 Leads for Free

### Step-by-Step Process

**Week 1: Gather raw company lists**

1. Create a free Apollo.io account. Use your 10,000 credits.
2. Run each of the 5 ICP segment searches above. Export 150-200 companies per segment.
3. Supplement with Google Maps scraping using Outscraper (free tier: 500 records) for local businesses.
4. For Ukrainian market, cross-reference with OpenDataBot for company verification.

**Week 2: Find and verify emails**

5. For companies without emails in Apollo, use Hunter.io domain search (25 free/month) and Snov.io (50 free/month).
6. Use LinkedIn profile URLs from Apollo to find personal email patterns.
7. Verify all emails through NeverBounce free tier (1,000 verifications) or Snov.io built-in verification.
8. Remove all catch-all domains and invalid emails. Target 90%+ verification rate.

**Week 3: Enrich and segment**

9. Add personalization data: company website URL, recent news, tech stack (use BuiltWith free lookups).
10. Tag each lead with their ICP segment (1-5) and tier (1-3).
11. Final list should be 500-1000 verified business emails, segmented and enriched.

**Expected yield**: Start with 1,500 raw companies, expect 60-70% to yield a valid business email = 900-1,050 leads.

## Domain Setup

### Buy a Separate Domain — Never Use Your Main Domain

Your main domain (agency-site-iota-rose.vercel.app or any custom domain) must never be used for cold email. If the cold domain gets flagged, your main domain stays clean.

**Domain naming convention** — pick something close to your brand but distinct:
- nexusai-team.com
- gonexusai.com
- nexusai.dev
- withnexusai.com

**Where to buy**: Namecheap, Cloudflare Registrar, or Google Domains. Cost: $10-15/year.

**Buy 2-3 domains** to rotate sending and reduce risk of any single domain getting burned.

### Set Up Email Accounts

For each domain, create 2-3 email accounts:
- alex@gonexusai.com
- team@gonexusai.com
- hello@nexusai-team.com

Use Google Workspace ($6/user/month) or Zoho Mail (free for up to 5 users, limited features).

---

## DNS Authentication: SPF, DKIM, DMARC

### SPF Record
Add a TXT record to your domain DNS:

```
Type: TXT
Host: @
Value: v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all
```
(Adjust the include based on your email provider. Above is for Google Workspace.)

### DKIM Record
Google Workspace generates this automatically:
1. Go to Google Admin > Apps > Google Workspace > Gmail > Authenticate Email
2. Click "Generate New Record"
3. Add the provided TXT record to your DNS

```
Type: TXT
Host: google._domainkey
Value: [generated by Google Workspace]
```

### DMARC Record
Start with a monitoring policy, then move to enforcement:

```
Type: TXT
Host: _dmarc
Value: v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@gonexusai.com; pct=100
```

After 2 weeks of clean sending, upgrade to:
```
v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@gonexusai.com; pct=100
```

### Verification
Use MXToolbox (mxtoolbox.com) to verify all three records are correctly configured before sending any emails.

## Email Warmup Process (2 Weeks Minimum)

### Why Warmup Matters
New domains have zero sender reputation. ISPs treat unknown senders as potential spam. Warmup builds trust gradually by simulating real email conversations.

### Warmup Schedule

| Day | Emails Sent | Emails Received (Warmup Replies) |
|-----|-------------|----------------------------------|
| 1-3 | 5/day | 3-5 replies/day |
| 4-7 | 10/day | 7-10 replies/day |
| 8-10 | 15/day | 10-15 replies/day |
| 11-14 | 20/day | 15-20 replies/day |

### Warmup Tools
- **Instantly.ai warmup** — Built-in, included in all plans. Connects to a network of real inboxes that exchange emails with yours.
- **Warmup Inbox** — Standalone tool, $15/month per inbox.
- **Lemwarm (by Lemlist)** — $29/month, integrates with Lemlist sequences.

**Important**: Keep warmup running even after you start sending campaigns. It maintains your reputation.

## A/B Testing Protocol

### What to Test (One Variable at a Time)

**Priority 1: Subject lines**
- Split your list 50/50
- Run each variant for minimum 100 sends
- Winner = higher open rate after 72 hours
- Test cadence: every 2 weeks

**Priority 2: Opening line**
- Same subject line for both variants
- Change only the first 1-2 sentences
- Winner = higher reply rate
- Test cadence: every 2 weeks

**Priority 3: CTA (call to action)**
- Test different CTAs: "15-minute call?" vs "want me to send a proposal?" vs "open to seeing a demo?"
- Winner = higher positive reply rate

**Priority 4: Send time**
- Test morning (8-10 AM) vs late morning (10 AM - 12 PM)
- Test Tuesday vs Thursday

### How to Run Tests in Instantly.ai
1. Create two variants of the email in the same campaign
2. Instantly automatically splits sending 50/50
3. After 100+ sends per variant, check the analytics dashboard
4. Pause the losing variant and let the winner run

## When to Pause and Adjust

### Red Flags — Pause Immediately

- **Open rate drops below 25%**: Your emails are hitting spam. Check: Is warmup still running? Are you exceeding send limits? Run a deliverability test with Mail-Tester (mail-tester.com).
- **Bounce rate exceeds 5%**: Your list quality is bad. Re-verify all emails. Remove the bounced addresses permanently.
- **Unsubscribe rate exceeds 2% per campaign**: Your targeting is off. Review your ICP filters — you are reaching people who do not see your offer as relevant.
- **Zero replies after 200+ sends**: Your messaging is not landing. Rewrite the opening email completely. Test a new angle.

### Yellow Flags — Adjust Within a Week

- **Reply rate between 1-3%**: Messaging is mediocre. A/B test new subject lines and opening lines.
- **High open rate (50%+) but low reply rate (below 3%)**: Subject line is working but the email body is not compelling. Rewrite the value proposition and CTA.
- **Positive replies but no meetings booked**: Your follow-up process is too slow or your booking flow is broken. Respond within 1 hour and check your Calendly link.

### Weekly Review Checklist

Every Monday morning, review:
1. Emails sent vs delivered (deliverability)
2. Open rates by campaign and by variant
3. Reply rates by campaign — categorize replies as positive, negative, or neutral
4. Meetings booked vs meetings held
5. Any new leads to add to sequences from signal monitoring

## GDPR Rules for Cold Email in the EU

### What Is Allowed

The GDPR does not outright ban cold B2B email. It is permitted under **legitimate interest** (Article 6(1)(f)) if you meet these conditions:

1. **You are emailing business addresses, not personal ones.** firstname@company.com is acceptable. firstname.lastname@gmail.com is not. Target professional email addresses at business domains only.

2. **You have a legitimate reason to contact them.** Your product or service must be genuinely relevant to their business. "We sell AI automation to e-commerce companies, and they are an e-commerce company" is a legitimate interest. Mass-blasting unrelated contacts is not.

3. **You identify yourself clearly.** Every email must include: your name, your company name, your company address, and a way to opt out.

4. **You provide an easy opt-out.** Every email must include a working unsubscribe link or clear instructions to opt out. Honor opt-outs immediately — within 24 hours at most.

5. **You do not email the same person after they opt out.** Maintain a suppression list. Before every campaign send, cross-reference your list against your suppression list.

### What Is Not Allowed

- Emailing personal (non-business) email addresses without consent
- Purchasing email lists from third parties without verification of consent
- Continuing to email after someone unsubscribes
- Hiding your identity or using deceptive subject lines
- Collecting or processing more data than necessary for the outreach

### Country-Specific Nuances

- **Germany**: Stricter interpretation. Double opt-in is the cultural norm. Cold B2B email is tolerated if clearly relevant, but expect higher unsubscribe rates. Always include a physical address and Impressum-equivalent information.
- **UK (post-Brexit)**: Follows UK GDPR + PECR. B2B cold email is allowed under legitimate interest. Must include clear opt-out.
- **Netherlands**: Generally follows standard GDPR. B2B email permitted under legitimate interest.
- **Poland**: Telecommunications Law requires consent for electronic marketing. B2B cold email exists in a gray area — keep volumes moderate and relevance high.
- **Nordics (Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Finland)**: Generally permissive for B2B cold email under legitimate interest. Nordic business culture values directness — keep emails brief and professional.

## Ukrainian Laws on Commercial Emails

### Relevant Legislation

Ukraine's Law on Electronic Commerce and the Law on Advertising regulate commercial emails:

1. **Consent is recommended but not strictly enforced for B2B.** Ukrainian law technically requires consent for advertising messages, but enforcement for B2B commercial email is minimal.
2. **Identify yourself.** Include your business name and contact information.
3. **Provide an opt-out mechanism.** Include a way to unsubscribe in every email.
4. **Do not send misleading content.** Subject lines and content must not be deceptive.

### Practical Approach for Ukrainian Market

- Use business email addresses only
- Include your company details in every email
- Add an unsubscribe option (even a simple "Reply STOP to unsubscribe")
- Maintain a suppression list
- Keep records of your lead sources
